Editorial

Compelled by the Fire: The Fourth Day

New Heart Ministry recently celebrated its 54th gathering in Korea, marking nearly two decades of God's undeniable grace across nations, including Cambodia, Vietnam, China, Ethiopia, Israel, and Korea. This movement is a divine summons igniting the promise of a New Heart (Ezekiel 36:26) in every generation and weaving us into one fierce, global Kingdom Family. Our commitment is comprehensive, extending to the New Heart Kids programs, which underscore our conviction that children are critical and active participants today, ensuring a legacy of passion that will sustain the movement for years to come.

The Fourth Day: Our Unstoppable Mandate

Our three-day gatherings are a deep immersion‚Äîa holy soaking in God's love and transformative power, but this is the preparation, not the end; we are utterly compelled to live the Fourth Day, which is the immediate, daily application of our radical, transformed hearts and our unstoppable mandate to reject spiritual stagnation and pursue sustained maturity in God's goodness and grace by remaining connected, to hold nothing back and serve in unified purpose (Acts 2) because isolation is not an option and our strength is in our unbreakable family covenant spanning continents, and to constantly commit to this ultimate purpose of raising a people of fire and fulfilling the global expansion God promised in Isaiah 54.

This Global Newsletter

As God's vision for New Heart Ministry continues to sharpen with undeniable clarity, we sense His urgent desire not only to document the mighty works and abundant grace flowing but also for us, the Family, to become more intimately intertwined and nurtured; hence, this quarterly Global Newsletter for the New Heart Family is a necessity, not an option, serving as the intentional tool designed to bridge the miles and cultures across the world to nurture global connections and ensure we care for one another as one body, to share the undeniable evidence of God's faithfulness from every region, solidifying our collective faith, and to keep our focus sharp, reminding us of our compulsion to live out the Fourth Day together and fostering sustained growth and maturity until the next gathering.

We must step forward. The Kingdom Family is not a passive recipient of grace, but an active force compelled by an unwavering vision. By His strength, we will fulfill every declaration over this ministry.
