Ariel ‚Äî Full Story

My sister's profound experience with New Heart 52 in Israel had a deep impact on me. Witnessing her transformation and her confession of faith in Jesus to our mother inspired me to attend New Heart in the USA in May 2025.

I was born into a devout Orthodox Jewish family, alongside my sister and our triplet brother. Our upbringing was steeped in the traditions and teachings of our faith, and for many years, this was the foundation of my religion. However, as I grew older, a sense of unease began to stir within me. Despite my fervent prayers and commitment to our religious practices, I found myself yearning for a deeper connection, a more profound experience of God's peace and love, which I felt was lacking.

This spiritual yearning led me to seek guidance, and I prayed earnestly for God to lead me to the truth. About six years ago, my journey took a transformative turn. I began to research Jesus, approaching the subject with an open mind and a sincere desire to understand. What I discovered during this period of intense study and reflection was nothing short of revelatory. I delved into the prophecies of the Hebrew Bible and was astonished to see how clearly they pointed to Jesus as the long-awaited Messiah. The intricate details, the historical accuracy, and the profound theological implications of these prophecies resonated deeply within me. It was a profound awakening, confirming a truth that had been quietly stirring in my heart. This journey of discovery solidified my belief in Jesus, forever changing my spiritual path.

Getting permission to leave Israel during my army service, especially with the ongoing war, was challenging. We booked the flight, prayed earnestly, and by God's grace, I received permission just one day before I departed for the USA.

The New Heart program was a period of immense grace and personal change. Over three days, I grew closer to God by spending undistracted time with Him, a profoundly enriching experience. I am grateful to God for transforming my life and giving me a new heart during New Heart 53.

Han, Ye-Sul ‚Äî Full Story

Hello. My name is Han Ye-seul, and I participated as a candidate in the 51st session, serving as a team member for the first time this time. Honestly, I wasn't sure if the accompaniment would be heard, so I kept playing soft background music (BGM) and hesitated to come forward. But since it's the final testimony, I decided to share. This is because every moment I served in New Heart's 54th session has been a great testimony for me.

When I participated as a candidate in the 51st session at my husband's suggestion, I kept asking, "What will we do? Where are we going? What's the schedule?" but no one told me anything. I had to go without any information, like boarding a deep-sea fishing vessel. Last year, it was during the weekdays, and since Wednesday was a workday, I traveled from Cheonan to Yeoncheon by train and subway after work, arriving around 11 p.m. I was told not to speak, and when I asked, "Why?" I didn't get an answer. As a candidate, I couldn't quite understand the terms they used, and though I was tired, the sessions were long. Despite struggles during the program, I experienced many awakenings and moments of repentance. My husband hadn't shared much about New Heart initially, which made me slightly resistant, but I didn't show it. At the end, I said, "I really enjoyed it. I want to serve as a team member next year."

When the announcement came this year, I eagerly asked my husband, "Did you apply? Did you apply?" Thankfully, he joined me in serving. While serving on the praise team this time, I found myself interceding more for the candidates than focusing on my own blessings. During prayer times, I wasn't able to pray much for myself because I was engaged in singing, playing the accompaniment, and serving in small ways.

On the third evening during prayer, I was standing by, ready to assist with water or tissues if needed. Toward the end, I just stood still. In that quiet moment, I felt as if God called out, "Ye-seul," in my heart. From that moment, I couldn't do anything but stand there with my hands raised in worship. God reminded me of Mary and Martha. While serving Jesus is important, what He truly desires is for us to sit at His feet and listen. I realized that while serving is good, I must first be present before Jesus, listening to His words. It led me to resolutely pray, "God, while serving is valuable, I will prioritize sitting before You. Your ministry is important, but I will stand before You first."

Before attending, I was worried about my physical condition. I had been resting at home, used to sleeping and eating leisurely. Suddenly waking up at 6 a.m., serving until late, and repeating that schedule concerned me. However, God's grace sustained me. Even with short sleep, God provided deep rest. Both physically and spiritually, God rejuvenated me.

I encourage all candidates to serve as team members. The grace you receive as a candidate differs from the grace experienced while serving as a team member. If you have the time and opportunity next year, I hope you participate. Thank you.

Keziah ‚Äî Full Story

Greetings, I am Keziah from Vietnam. I sincerely thank God for the opportunity to attend the 14th New Heart retreat, where I was visited by God miraculously and powerfully. Throughout the retreat, I experienced deep communion with Him, completely undistracted.

I have followed the Lord for 14 years, and during that time, I have always felt His boundless love for me. Because of that love, I chose to respond by serving Him with all my heart. However, over time, I realized that I often served with the desire to be recognized by Him, without truly understanding His love for me completely.

On the first night of the retreat, I wept before the Lord and asked for His forgiveness for letting my thoughts control His work instead of following His will. I understood that God's will is that He loves me not because of what I do, but simply because He loves me. Whether I do anything or not, His love is always unconditional for me.

His Word in Jeremiah 32:40 freed me from being bound by my own righteousness, and since then, I have lived in true freedom, believing in His love and plan for my life. He revealed Himself by using me to convey His love to many others. Since the retreat, I have prayed with many who have come to faith in Jesus and are now being discipled.

Thank God, glory belongs to Him.

Parichat ‚Äî Full Story

I, Parichat, would like to express heartfelt thanks to Spiritual Mother Pastor Mayuri, Pastor Sarah, and Pastor Matthew for their guidance, care, and love from the very first day we arrived in Korea until the very last day of the mission.

Thank you for the precious experiences and blessings I received through joining the "New Heart" mission in Korea. I was deeply impressed by the New Heart mission, from the warm transportation arrangement to pick up our team, the loving welcome through songs of praise, the joyful meal team filled with laughter, the worship team overflowing with the Holy Spirit, and the prayer team that continually covered us throughout the mission.

I received deep inner restoration and have firmly decided to serve and draw even closer to God. I also chose to forgive my ex-husband for everything that had happened in the past and even in the present. The accusations and hatred that I carried for many years have now turned into something new that leads me to pray even more.

Before joining the mission, I had carried pain in my heart for more than 18 years. But now, that burden has become so much lighter, and I can truly feel that I am no longer carrying it the way I once did. New Heart 54 has been a great blessing and transformation for both my heart and my spirit.

Ji Yeo-woon ‚Äî New Heart Kids Korea #2

New Heart Kids Korea #2 brought together 22 children and 10 teachers in the same location as the adult gathering, yet on a carefully separate schedule. What began with nerves and newness turned into a powerful time where children worshiped, prayed, and even asked to come again next year.

Kyungjin (Maria) Park ‚Äî New Heart Kids USA #1

In Los Angeles, New Heart Kids USA #1 was birthed out of a longing to see parents and children encounter God together. As young parents received deep restoration in the main gathering, their children were equally met by God and envisioned as future leaders of the New Heart family.

Ji Yeo-woon ‚Äî Full Story

Hello! I'm Ji Yeo-woon, who served as the coordinator for New Heart Kids. This year, New Heart Kids was held in Korea for the first time, and although we were in the same location as the adult program, we mostly adjusted schedules so we wouldn't cross paths.

This year, New Heart Kids had 22 children (ages 0‚Äì12) and 10 teachers. Everything was new, so both teachers and children were nervous. On the first night, we asked the children why they came. Answers included, "To worship," "To pray," "To play," and one child said, "To do missions!" It was profound.

New Heart Kids was full of discomfort ‚Äî sleeping in unfamiliar places, being separated from parents, mixed ages, phones removed, strict TV limits, and rain that cancelled outdoor play. Despite all of this, the children endured, followed instructions, and many even cried when it was time to go home, asking if they could come again next year.

We prepared many programs, but more than half never happened because leading the children safely from place to place took longer than expected. But God's plan was fully accomplished. The children prayed, worshiped, heard the Word, and experienced God in ways that surprised even us. We truly saw the Holy Spirit holding everything together.

In the end, teachers shared only gratitude ‚Äî despite exhaustion, babies, pregnancy, and physical difficulties ‚Äî because grace overflowed. Many even said, "Next year!" without hesitation. We believe God is building the body of Christ even among the little ones, and we look forward to what He will do next.

Kyungjin (Maria) Park ‚Äî Full Story

In May 2025, God opened a beautiful new chapter as we launched New Heart Kids #1 in Los Angeles. It had been a long-awaited dream within New Heart to see all generations blessed together, and God opened the way.

As a mother of four, I remembered the years when I longed for spiritual renewal but could not join fully because I was raising little children. My heart was especially for young parents ‚Äî I wanted to serve them so they could receive restoration in the Lord.

But as we served, God expanded my heart to love their children deeply. Serving about twenty children, from toddlers to preteens, I saw how much God treasures every single one. He desires to raise and equip them as future leaders within the New Heart family.

By the end of the three days, both parents and children had received a new heart through the Holy Spirit. When we later heard that New Heart Kids #2 had launched in Korea, we felt God confirming His heart ‚Äî that He is calling us as a family, to pass His New Heart to all generations.

Editorial

Compelled by Fire: The New Heart Family Living the Fourth Day

New Heart Ministry recently celebrated its 54th gathering in Korea, marking nearly two decades of God's undeniable grace across nations, including Cambodia, Vietnam, China, Ethiopia, Israel, and Korea. This movement is a divine summons igniting the promise of a New Heart (Ezekiel 36:26) in every generation and weaving us into one fierce, global Kingdom Family. Our commitment is comprehensive, extending to the New Heart Kids programs, which underscore our conviction that children are critical and active participants today, ensuring a legacy of passion that will sustain the movement for years to come.

Our three-day gatherings are a deep immersion‚Äîa holy soaking in God's love and transformative power, but this is the preparation, not the end; we are utterly compelled to live the Fourth Day, which is the immediate, daily application of our radical, transformed hearts and our unstoppable mandate to reject spiritual stagnation and pursue sustained maturity in God's goodness and grace by remaining connected, to hold nothing back and serve in unified purpose (Acts 2). Our strength is in our unbreakable family covenant spanning continents, and we are constantly committing to this ultimate purpose of raising a people of fire and fulfilling the global expansion God promised in Isaiah 54.

As God‚Äôs vision for New Heart Ministry continues to sharpen with undeniable clarity, we sense His urgent desire not only to document the mighty works and abundant grace flowing but also for us, the Family, to become more intimately intertwined and nurtured; hence, this quarterly Global Newsletter for the New Heart Family is a necessity, not an option, serving as the intentional tool designed to bridge the miles and cultures across the world to nurture global connections and ensure we care for one another as one body, to share the undeniable evidence of God's faithfulness from every region, solidifying our collective faith, and to keep our focus sharp, reminding us of our compulsion to live out the Fourth Day together and fostering sustained growth and maturity until the next gathering. We must step forward. The Kingdom Family is not a passive recipient of grace, but an active force compelled by an unwavering vision. By His strength, we will fulfill every declaration over this ministry.
