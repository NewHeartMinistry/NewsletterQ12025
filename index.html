Ariel

NH USA #53 â€” Candidate

My sister's profound experience with New Heart 52 in Israel deeply impacted me. Witnessing her transformation and her confession of faith in Jesus to our mother inspired me to participate in New Heart in the United States in May 2025.

I was born into a devoutly religious Orthodox Jewish family, alongside my sister and our triplet brother. Our upbringing was steeped in the traditions and teachings of our faith, and for many years, this was the foundation of my religion. However, as I grew older, a sense of disquiet began to stir within me. Despite my fervent prayers and commitment to our religious practices, I found myself longing for a deeper connection, a more profound experience of God's peace and love, which I felt was missing.

This spiritual yearning led me to seek guidance, and I prayed earnestly that God would lead me to the truth. About six years ago, my journey took a revolutionary turn. I began exploring Jesus, approaching the subject with an open mind and a genuine willingness to understand. What I discovered during this period of intense study and contemplation was nothing short of revelatory. I delved into the prophecies of the Hebrew Bible and was astonished to see how clearly they pointed to Jesus as the awaited Messiah. The intricate details, the historical accuracy, and the profound theological implications of these prophecies resonated deeply within me. It was a profound awakening that confirmed a truth that had been quietly growing in my heart. This journey of discovery solidified my faith in Jesus, forever altering my spiritual path.

Gaining approval to leave Israel during my military service, especially with the ongoing war, was challenging. We booked the flight, prayed earnestly, and by God's grace, I received approval just one day before I left for the United States.

The New Heart program was a period of immense grace and personal transformation. Over three days, I drew closer to God by spending time with Him, hearing His Word, and worshiping in community. I experienced deep healing and freedom from past wounds. The testimonies of others moved me profoundly, and I felt the Holy Spirit working powerfully in my life. By the end, I felt renewed in my faith, equipped with a stronger foundation, and ready to live more boldly for Christ.

Choi Eun-Young

NH Korea #54 â€” Candidate

1. Gratitude
First, I cannot forget the spiritual leaders who delivered the Word, the praise team who gave their body and soul, the Palanca team who stayed up all night making many things with devotion, those who shared love through food and snacks, the general affairs team who handled all the troublesome work behind the scenes so the gathering could proceed, and above all, the Royo members who shared their entire lives with tears and love, and each tearful prayer offered on their knees during the foot-washing ceremony. I am truly grateful. I received a deep and profound love that one cannot receive in life.

2. Situation Before Going
I received an invitation to attend this retreat from a pastor's wife starting a year ago. She had been praying for me for over a year. Then this year, I received another invitation to attend the retreat. I strongly felt God inviting me to this gathering through someone. So this time I made up my mind to "go with full determination." However, one week before attendance, my family members who love and care for me deeply were absolutely opposed to my attending the retreat. They asked how I could go to a gathering where neither the speakers nor the schedule were shared.

However, I had two desperate reasons why I absolutely had to attend the retreat this time. One was that my husband was graduating from theological seminary this year while simultaneously retiring from his company, and I had been continuously asking God for direction on how we should live our lives from next year. The other was that I saw myself having lost energy in both body and soul and being exhausted after the COVID period.

3. Grace Received
From the moment I arrived at New Heart until I left, what I experienced was "the hospitality and deep love of those who had fervently experienced God's grace." On the first day, during the first lecture on "vision," God had me comprehensively reflect on my life. He warmly asked me, "Eun-young, how are you using your money, time, and talents?" A few years ago, I started experiencing numbness in my hands, followed by my feet. My joints throughout my body began to ache. When I went to the hospital, I was diagnosed with progressive arthritis and polyneuropathy. The doctor told me that this disease has no cure and would continue to worsen. But at the New Heart retreat, God asked me: "So do you want to live the remaining days of your life only for yourself?"

During the retreat, I prayed and worshiped with abandonment, sang loudly, and cried deeply. Through this, God touched me physically. The joint pain significantly decreased, and the numbness improved noticeably. Beyond physical healing, God rekindled a passion in my heart to live boldly for His kingdom despite my limitations. I left New Heart with renewed purpose: to steward my remaining days for God's glory and the good of others.

2) During the Retreat

As we sang "I Stand," old wounds resurfaced, yet I chose to walk into them again with the joy of salvation. Pastor Kim said, "If you try your three-day answer and fail, just start another three days." I will seek the Spirit's leading day by day through the Word and prayer.
