Ariel ‚Äî Full Story

My sister's profound experience with New Heart 52 in Israel had a deep impact on me. Witnessing her transformation and her confession of faith in Jesus to our mother inspired me to attend New Heart in the USA in May 2025.

I was born into a devout Orthodox Jewish family, alongside my sister and our triplet brother. Our upbringing was steeped in the traditions and teachings of our faith, and for many years, this was the foundation of my religion. However, as I grew older, a sense of unease began to stir within me. Despite my fervent prayers and commitment to our religious practices, I found myself yearning for a deeper connection, a more profound experience of God's peace and love, which I felt was lacking.

This spiritual yearning led me to seek guidance, and I prayed earnestly for God to lead me to the truth. About six years ago, my journey took a transformative turn. I began to research Jesus, approaching the subject with an open mind and a sincere desire to understand. What I discovered during this period of intense study and reflection was nothing short of revelatory. I delved into the prophecies of the Hebrew Bible and was astonished to see how clearly they pointed to Jesus as the long-awaited Messiah. The intricate details, the historical accuracy, and the profound theological implications of these prophecies resonated deeply within me. It was a profound awakening, confirming a truth that had been quietly stirring in my heart. This journey of discovery solidified my belief in Jesus, forever changing my spiritual path.

Getting permission to leave Israel during my army service, especially with the ongoing war, was challenging. We booked the flight, prayed earnestly, and by God's grace, I received permission just one day before I departed for the USA.

The New Heart program was a period of immense grace and personal change. Over three days, I grew closer to God by spending undistracted time with Him, a profoundly enriching experience. I am grateful to God for transforming my life and giving me a new heart during New Heart 53.

Newheart 54th Generation Hallelujah Group - Choi Eun-young

1. Gratitude

First, I cannot forget the spiritual leaders who delivered the Word, the praise team who gave their body and soul, the Palanca team who stayed up all night making many things with devotion, those who shared love through food and snacks, the general affairs team who handled all the troublesome work behind the scenes so the gathering could proceed, and above all, the Royo members who shared their entire lives with tears and love, and each tearful prayer offered on their knees during the foot-washing ceremony. I am truly grateful. I received a profound love that one cannot receive in life.

2. Situation Before Going

I received an invitation to attend this retreat from a pastor's wife a year ago. She had been praying for me for over a year. Then this year, I received another invitation to attend the retreat. I strongly felt God inviting me to this gathering through someone. This time, I made up my mind to "go with full determination." However, one week before the retreat, my family members, who love and care for me deeply, were absolutely opposed to my attending. They asked how I could go to a gathering where neither the speakers nor the schedule were shared. They even joked that "it could be a deep-sea fishing boat going to gut anchovies."

However, I had two desperate reasons why I absolutely had to attend the retreat this time. One was that my husband was graduating from a theological seminary this year, while simultaneously retiring from his company. I had been continuously asking God for direction on how we should live our lives from next year, so I needed spiritual wisdom and strength to discern this. The other was that I felt I had lost energy in both body and soul, and was exhausted after the COVID period. So, I wanted to reflect on my life through the church community, having lost my way, and stand up again.

3. Grace Received

From the moment I arrived at Newheart until I left, what I experienced was "the hospitality and deep love of those who had fervently experienced God's grace." On the first day, during the first lecture on "vision," God had me comprehensively reflect on my life. He warmly asked me, "Eun-young, how are you using your money, time, and talents?" A few years ago, I started experiencing numbness in my legs and paralysis in my arms due to neck and back disc problems. I was so scared that I significantly reduced my essential work, which included reading books and papers, as well as organizing counseling cases. Instead, I spent time on Naver and YouTube searches. Naturally, the books and papers I wanted to write, and the programs I wanted to create, gradually moved further away.

When I was young, life was rough and challenging because I was very poor and had to work a lot. However, as retirement approached, life became comfortable, and a feeling of "it would be nice just to continue like this" gradually crept in. So sometimes I would ask God, "God, isn't this enough? Can't I now rest comfortably and live according to my nature?" My nature is that of an "adult with ADHD tendencies who loves meeting and playing with people." Looking back on my life, I was someone very, very far from research and education. "Ah, since I've lived struggling with my own strength, I can rest now"‚Äîthis feeling honestly came to me quite often.

However, from the first lecture on "vision" through the 11 Royo testimonies that followed, God allowed me to hear with "tears in my heart" how the Royistas He sent were overcoming their deficiencies and life's adversities through their love and devotion to God. It felt like hearing "Hebrews chapter 11" in heaven. God asked me each time: your fellow workers in faith, your seniors in faith, are fighting this fight of faith with difficulty but victoriously. What fight of faith do you want to fight until the moment you die?

And God gently rebuked my laziness and indolence, my experience of judging and analyzing people through long periods of counseling and the resulting fear of being judged, and my life that had stopped and was resting because of the criticism I had heard for a long time from the counseling field and the church as a Christian counseling scholar, and called me back to the fierce spiritual battlefield.

Another challenge I experienced at the retreat was related to speaking in tongues. As a Presbyterian believer accustomed only to quiet, silent prayer, I was reminded of ministry workers from childhood Sunday school retreats who forced tongues on me regardless of my will, saying you receive tongues by loudly repeating various gum brand names, and the loud tongues prayers of people next to me during the entire retreat and group prayer times made it even harder for me to concentrate on prayer.

Finally, during group prayer time, I told the group leader that I was terrified and uncomfortable with tongues prayer. Unfortunately, my prayer time became a venue for spiritual debate about "tongues prayer." However, during this time, I became much more open and came to long for the work of the Holy Spirit, as manifested in the form of "tongues." If I had been living with my own thoughts and plans first for a long time, I would have decided to open myself more and surrender more to the work of the Holy Spirit during this time.

4. After Finishing

As the retreat concluded, we sang the theme song, "I Stand Up, I Will No Longer Let Pain Be Painful," and the wounds and hurts I had received in various forms from the world and the church, while living as a pastor's daughter and Christian counselor, came flooding back to me. I made a tearful decision to return to those wounds and hurts, but go with the love and joy of the cross's salvation that God reconfirmed to me at the retreat.

After writing a list of sins, nailing and burning them on the cross, Pastor Kim's words keep staying in my heart: "If you live according to your 3-day repentance and fail, you can just start another 3 days." Through the Newheart retreat, I will try to live according to the stories God told me, sometimes powerfully, sometimes in detail. I will receive the Holy Spirit's guidance day by day through daily reading of the Word and prayer to find my future path and calling.

Whenever I become lazy and indolent, I will recall Christ's sacrificial love, who did not spare His life on the cross for me, and the fellow workers in faith, the Royos, who are living this out with their whole bodies. And I will live another 3 days.

I sincerely give thanks and glory to all team members who gave their time, hearts, all talents, and efforts to make all this possible, and to God who poured this grace upon me from behind the scenes. De colores.

Ji, Yeowoon ‚Äî Full Story

Hello! I'm Ji Yeo-woon, and I served with Newheart Kids.

This year, Newheart Kids was held in Korea for the first time, and although we were in the same location, I think you probably know almost nothing about what we were doing since we had to adjust schedules to avoid running into each other üôÇ

This year, Newheart Kids had a total of 22 children from ages 0 to 12, along with 10 teachers, spending time together. Since everything was a first, it wasn't just the teachers who were nervous and worried. The first day we met together, even the children who had to spend the entire day away from their guardians had tense faces.

After eating dinner, we gathered to introduce ourselves and greet each other, then asked the question, "What kind of place is this? Why did we come here today?" While answers like "To worship!" "To pray!" "To play!" came up, one child shouted, "To do missions!" It was an answer that provided great insight.

It wouldn't be an exaggeration to say that Newheart Kids was full of discomfort. Of course, the same goes for adult Newheart. Let me give you some examples of what I mean. First, the sleeping arrangements change. You have to stay in a place that's a bit uncomfortable rather than cozy, with people you're seeing for the first time. You can't control your own schedule. Although the meals and refreshments are excellent, it's not a system where you can choose and order the menu you want to eat.

Let me approach this from the children's perspective. You might want to be quiet, but the surroundings can be noisy, and you might want to run, but sometimes you have to sit still. Because various ages are mixed together, you can't behave as you would with friends, and you always need to be careful. You have to wake up earlier than usual, and from morning to evening, even after eating meals, you can't see your mom or dad, and even strangers might bathe you.

For elementary school students, they even had to turn in their phones. There's a TV right in front of you, but it doesn't turn on unless it's the designated time. Even then, all the content you can watch is only videos related to Jesus. There were many things you could do at home that you couldn't do here. It even rained continuously, so we couldn't do outdoor activities. The list could go on endlessly.

What did the children do? The children surprisingly complied with instructions and endured many situations. Of course, this doesn't mean they "obediently and happily listened." There were naturally children who said they didn't like something when they didn't, asked "why?", asked for something different, or acted according to their own stubbornness. Some cried, saying they missed their mom.

Nevertheless, isn't it truly amazing that the children managed to survive 3 nights and 4 days together without any safety incidents? If the children hadn't been obedient, we really couldn't have made it to the end. At the end, they were sad about going home and even said, "Are we doing this again next year? I want to come again!"

While preparing Newheart Kids, the teachers had the idea that we needed to "teach something for the children to remember." We heard a lot from the leadership that Newheart Kids is not simply "childcare." So we really worked hard to design, plan, and prepare programs, but except for delivering the Word, it's hard to say things went properly.

More than half of the small/large group activities and games we prepared for the children weren't even taken out. We didn't know that the distance of taking them to the chapel one by one under umbrellas would be so far. Adjusting time and avoiding adult candidates so we wouldn't run into them also required quite a bit of attention. At least from a human perspective, one could evaluate that things didn't go according to plan.

However, I believe that God's plan was fully accomplished. Personally, I think these 3 nights and 4 days of children rubbing shoulders with each other might have been God's plan itself. Children living in modern society today would have almost no opportunity to experience this kind of environment. Especially for preschool children, even less so. I think it must have been a huge challenge for all the children.

Also, despite being physically separated from the adults, it was very moving that we often felt we were tied together as one with adult Newheart, going together. Do you remember when the children sang the song "Grace"? Actually, none of us properly participated in the Royo Room worship, so we never imagined that adult candidates would sing this song, but when we went later, this song was on the list. As an aside, it seems really cute to have mostly 6‚Äì9-year-olds and at most 12-year-olds singing the Grace song. üòÅ

What do you think? Don't you think we really went "to do missions"? Newheart Kids spent time being grateful for what was given, even in truly insufficient, uncomfortable, and poor conditions, laughing and enjoying each other, and rubbing shoulders closely. They learned to pray, sang praises, heard and shared the Word, learned worship, and spent every day in God.

I think all these things were possible because the children followed along so well. Especially, I'm certain that without the Holy Spirit being with us, all of this would have been impossible, and they would never have wanted to come back to such an uncomfortable place.

You've probably heard many times the phrase "Looking back after Newheart ended, everything was grace." It was the same for us. A sister who had to care for her 4 & 5-year-old son and daughter while pregnant with her third child, a sister who returned as a Newheart team member after 13 years but couldn't go anywhere because she came with her 12-month-old child who was shy with strangers, a sister who had to deliver the Word to children while holding her child because the child severely refused to be separated from mom, a mother who could barely leave room 101 because she was cooking for the children as a first-time team member, dads who had to rush in to bathe boys while playing outside, etc...

I think not a single one of our team members was in an easy position. Nevertheless, when we shared feedback at the end, instead of anyone complaining about the poor environment, stories of gratitude and grace were exchanged. Especially from my perspective, I was grateful to everyone who willingly said "okay," even when I suddenly visited and asked them to do something, such as changing the schedule order or squeezing in an unplanned event.

There were no team members blaming others. We encouraged, motivated, and showed consideration for one another while acknowledging each other's weaknesses. In the way we shared feelings of being moved by each other's actions, I could feel that we were truly united in one heart. I'm grateful that we seemed to have a living experience of becoming the Lord's beautiful body.

And above all, all the teachers naturally talked about next plans to improve what was lacking this time, saying "Next year~". I'm promoting for many to come next year as a Kids team that wants to do this again. üòÜ
