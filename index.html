Ariel ‚Äî From Orthodox Judaism to Jesus

My sister's profound experience with New Heart 52 in Israel had a deep impact on me. Witnessing her transformation and her confession of faith in Jesus to our mother inspired me to attend New Heart in the USA in May 2025.

I was born into a devout Orthodox Jewish family, alongside my sister and our triplet brother. Our upbringing was steeped in the traditions and teachings of our faith, and for many years, this was the foundation of my religion. However, as I grew older, a sense of unease began to stir within me. Despite my fervent prayers and commitment to our religious practices, I found myself yearning for a deeper connection, a more profound experience of God's peace and love, which I felt was lacking.

This spiritual yearning led me to seek guidance, and I prayed earnestly for God to lead me to the truth. About six years ago, my journey took a transformative turn. I began to research Jesus, approaching the subject with an open mind and a sincere desire to understand. What I discovered during this period of intense study and reflection was nothing short of revelatory.

I delved into the prophecies of the Hebrew Bible and was astonished to see how clearly they pointed to Jesus as the long-awaited Messiah. The intricate details, the historical accuracy, and the profound theological implications of these prophecies resonated deeply within me. It was a profound awakening, confirming a truth that had been quietly stirring in my heart. This journey of discovery solidified my belief in Jesus, forever changing my spiritual path.

Getting permission to leave Israel during my army service, especially with the ongoing war, was challenging. We booked the flight, prayed earnestly, and by God's grace, I received permission just one day before I departed for the USA.

The New Heart program was a period of immense grace and personal change. Over three days, I grew closer to God by spending undistracted time with Him, a profoundly enriching experience. I am grateful to God for transforming my life and giving me a new heart during New Heart 53.

Choi Eun-Young ‚Äî Called Back to the Fight

Gratitude: First, I cannot forget the spiritual leaders who delivered the Word, the praise team who gave their body and soul, the Palanca team who stayed up all night making many things with devotion, those who shared love through food and snacks, the general affairs team who handled all the troublesome work behind the scenes so the gathering could proceed, and above all, the Royo members who shared their entire lives with tears and love, and each tearful prayer offered on their knees during the foot-washing ceremony. I am truly grateful. I received a profound love that one cannot receive in life.

Situation Before Going: I received an invitation to attend this retreat from a pastor's wife a year ago. She had been praying for me for over a year. Then this year, I received another invitation to attend the retreat. I strongly felt God inviting me to this gathering through someone. This time, I made up my mind to "go with full determination." However, one week before the retreat, my family members, who love and care for me deeply, were absolutely opposed to my attending.

However, I had two desperate reasons why I absolutely had to attend the retreat this time. One was that my husband was graduating from a theological seminary this year, while simultaneously retiring from his company. I had been continuously asking God for direction on how we should live our lives from next year. The other was that I felt I had lost energy in both body and soul, and was exhausted after the COVID period.

Grace Received: From the moment I arrived at New Heart until I left, what I experienced was "the hospitality and deep love of those who had fervently experienced God's grace." On the first day, during the first lecture on "vision," God had me comprehensively reflect on my life. He warmly asked me, "Eun-young, how are you using your money, time, and talents?" A few years ago, I started experiencing numbness in my legs and paralysis in my arms due to neck and back disc problems.

God asked me each time: your fellow workers in faith, your seniors in faith, are fighting this fight of faith with difficulty but victoriously. What fight of faith do you want to fight until the moment you die? And God gently rebuked my laziness and indolence, and called me back to the fierce spiritual battlefield.

After Finishing: As the retreat concluded, we sang the theme song, "I Stand Up," and the wounds and hurts I had received came flooding back. I made a tearful decision to return to those wounds, but go with the love and joy of the cross's salvation that God reconfirmed to me at the retreat. I will live another 3 days. De colores.

Han Ye-Sul ‚Äî From Candidate to Intercessor

Hello. My name is Han Ye-seul, and I served as a team member for the first time in the 54th session, having previously participated as a candidate in the 51st session. Every moment of serving in New Heart's 54th session has been a profound testimony for me.

The first thing I felt was the need to grow through learning. After finishing New Heart #51 as a candidate, I felt deeply grateful to God for the amazing grace I experienced. At the same time, I started to think about how I should give back this grace I received.

About two years passed, and it became time for me to serve as a team member. However, I fell into a trap. Just as I had felt when preparing for Palanca as a candidate, I began to think that I should serve as a team member with some special zeal or devotion. But now, with a changed mindset, I realize that serving as a team member is not about doing something special or showing passionate devotion, but rather about offering what I have naturally, just as I am.

Secondly, I realized that God's work is so creative. God really uses each person's gifts and talents so well! Watching the team members serve in their respective roles made me realize again and again that God is the Creator who made us all different. Because of this, I no longer have feelings of jealousy or comparison. I was just grateful and overjoyed that I could be one of His creation and be used by God in this place.

Thirdly, I learned about a life of faith that perseveres. Through New Heart, I received deep teaching that the Christian life is a journey of going forward while looking backward, enduring and persevering. Pastor Kim's words, "If you try your 3-day repentance and fail, just start another 3 days," deeply touched my heart. These words will be my support on the tough days ahead, allowing me to continue taking one step at a time.

Fourthly, I realize that the blessing God gives is being together as a community. I was so happy to be able to go forward together as one body with many people who have the same faith, the same vision, and the same heart. Even though we are all so different, I learned that we can become one in Christ.

I sincerely thank all the team members who worked together. And I give all glory to God who allowed me to experience His grace once again through New Heart #54. De Colores!

Ji Yeowoon ‚Äî New Heart Kids Korea #2

Hello! I'm Ji Yeowoon, who served as the general affairs coordinator for New Heart Kids. This year, New Heart Kids was conducted in Korea for the first time, and although we were in the same location, we adjusted our schedules to avoid running into each other, so I think you probably had no idea what we were up to üôÇ

This year, New Heart Kids had a total of 22 children, ages 0 to 12, along with 10 teachers spending time together. Since everything was a first, it wasn't just the teachers who felt nervous. The children, who had spent the entire day away from their guardians, also met us on that first day, tension written on their faces.

After dinner, we gathered to introduce ourselves and greet each other, then asked the questions: "What is this place for? Why did we come here today?" Answers like "To worship!" "To pray!" "To play!" came out, and then one child shouted, "To do missions!" It was an answer that gave us great insight.

It wouldn't be an exaggeration to say that New Heart Kids was full of discomfort. The sleeping arrangements change. Stay with people you've never met before in places that are uncomfortable. You can't control your own schedule. Elementary school students had to turn in their cell phones. It even rained continuously so that we couldn't do outdoor activities.

What did the children do? Amazingly, the children complied with instructions and endured many situations. Of course, this doesn't mean they "happily and obediently listened." When they didn't like something, they said so, sometimes asked "Why?", asked for something different. Some cried because they missed their moms. Nevertheless, isn't it truly amazing that the children made it through all 3 nights and 4 days together without any safety incidents! At the end, they were sad about going home and even said, "Will you do this again next year? I want to come again!"

As we prepared for New Heart Kids, the teachers had the mindset that we needed to "teach the children something to remember." We really worked hard to design, plan, and prepare programs, but aside from delivering the Word, it's hard to say things went properly. More than half of the activities we prepared never even came out.

However, I believe that God's plan was completely fulfilled. Personally, I think these 3 nights and 4 days of children rubbing shoulders might have been God's plan. Children living in modern society today rarely have opportunities to experience this kind of environment. It must have been a really big challenge for all the children.

Also, even though we were physically separated from the adults, it was profoundly moving to feel often that we were tied together as one with adult New Heart. Do you remember the children singing "Grace"? As an aside, most of them were 6-9 years old, with the oldest being 12, so singing the "Grace" hymn seems really cute üòÅ

What do you think? Don't you think we really went "on a mission"? New Heart Kids spent time being grateful for what was given, even in truly lacking, uncomfortable, and poor conditions, laughing and enjoying together, and rubbing shoulders closely. They learned to pray, sang praises, heard and shared the Word, learned worship, and spent all their days in God.

You've probably heard many times the phrase, "Looking back after New Heart ended, everything was grace." It was the same for us. Every single one of our team members was in a not-easy situation. Nevertheless, when we shared feedback at the end, we exchanged words of gratitude and grace. We encouraged, motivated, and showed consideration for one another. We were truly united in one heart.

I'm grateful that we had a living experience of becoming the beautiful body of the Lord. And above all, all the teachers naturally talked about their next plans to improve what was lacking, saying "Next year~". I'm promoting for many to come next year!

Kyungjin (Maria) Park ‚Äî New Heart Kids USA #1

In May 2025, God opened a beautiful new chapter as we launched New Heart Kids #1 in Los Angeles. It was a long-awaited dream within the New Heart community to see all generations blessed through New Heart, and it was incredible to witness how God opened the door to make this dream a reality.

When I first joined New Heart Kids, my heart was especially for young parents. As a mother of four, I remembered the seasons when I felt spiritually dry and left out while raising my little ones. I wanted to serve in a way that would give parents space to be renewed and restored in God's presence.

But as I served, God expanded my heart to embrace their children as well. Serving about twenty children, from toddlers to preteens, I saw firsthand how deeply God treasures each one of these young children. He desires to raise and equip them as future leaders within the New Heart community.

There were certainly challenging moments, but by the end of those three days, I witnessed both parents and children receiving a new heart through the Holy Spirit. With the same heart and encouragement, I was overjoyed to hear that we would have New Heart Kids #2 in Korea in October.

I believe this is God's confirmation of His will to call us as a 'family' and to give His New Heart to all generations. I am grateful and full of faith for what lies ahead!
